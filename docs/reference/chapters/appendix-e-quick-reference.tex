\section{Instruction Mnemonic Index}
\label{sec:qr-mnemonic-index}
% ==========================================

All instruction mnemonics in alphabetical order. \emph{Type}: ALU, Memory (Mem), Control-flow (CF), Coprocessor (COP),
or Meta. \emph{Flags}: flags written by the instruction (\texttt{--} = none; \texttt{*} = determined by the
coprocessor). Meta-instructions inherit the flag behaviour of the underlying CPU instruction. For full encoding
details, see the chapter cited in the header of each group.

\section{Status Register Bit Reference}
\label{sec:qr-sr-bits}
% ==========================================

The status register (\reg{sr}) is 16 bits wide. The upper byte (bits 15--8) is privileged: reads in protected mode
return zero for those bits; direct writes in protected mode raise a privilege violation fault.

\begin{table}[H]
	\centering
	\small
	\begin{tabular}{clllp{6cm}}
		\toprule
		\textbf{Bit} & \textbf{Name} & \textbf{Access} & \textbf{Reset} & \textbf{Function}                                                                 \\
		\midrule
		15           & T             & Privileged      & 0              & Trace mode: fault after every instruction (used by debuggers)                     \\
		14           & A             & Privileged      & 0              & Address overflow trap: fault when an address calculation overflows                \\
		13           & EA            & Privileged      & 0              & Exception Active: set automatically while inside an exception handler (read-only) \\
		12           & E4            & Privileged      & 0              & Enable hardware interrupt line 4 (level 4, highest maskable)                      \\
		11           & E3            & Privileged      & 0              & Enable hardware interrupt line 3                                                  \\
		10           & E2            & Privileged      & 0              & Enable hardware interrupt line 2                                                  \\
		9            & E1            & Privileged      & 0              & Enable hardware interrupt line 1 (level 1, lowest maskable)                       \\
		8            & P             & Privileged      & 0              & Protected mode: 0 = supervisor, 1 = protected                                     \\
		\midrule
		7--4         & --            & Either          & 0              & Reserved; reads as 0                                                              \\
		\midrule
		3            & V             & Either          & 0              & Overflow: signed arithmetic overflow occurred                                     \\
		2            & C             & Either          & 0              & Carry: unsigned carry out or borrow                                               \\
		1            & N             & Either          & 0              & Negative: result bit 15 = 1 (two's complement sign bit)                           \\
		0            & Z             & Either          & 0              & Zero: result was zero                                                             \\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabular}
	\caption{Status Register Bit Summary}
	\label{tab:sr-bit-summary}
\end{table}

Hardware interrupt line 5 (level 5) is a Non-Maskable Interrupt (NMI) and is always enabled; it has no enable bit in
\reg{sr}. See Chapter~\ref{ch:status-register} for full details and examples.

\section{Bus Access Type (BAT) Reference}
\label{sec:qr-bat}
% ==========================================

The 3-bit Bus Access Type field is driven onto the BAT pins during every bus cycle. External devices (memory
controllers, coprocessors) can inspect these pins to determine the nature of the access.

\begin{table}[H]
	\centering
	\begin{tabular}{clp{7.5cm}}
		\toprule
		\textbf{BAT[2:0]} & \textbf{Value} & \textbf{Meaning}                            \\
		\midrule
		000               & 0              & No I/O (internal cycle, no bus transaction) \\
		001               & 1              & Instruction Fetch                           \\
		010               & 2              & Data Read                                   \\
		011               & 3              & Data Write                                  \\
		100               & 4              & Exception Vector Fetch                      \\
		101               & 5              & DMA Co-processor Read Burst                 \\
		110               & 6              & DMA Co-processor Write Burst                \\
		111               & 7              & Reserved                                    \\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabular}
	\caption{Bus Access Type (BAT) Encoding}
	\label{tab:bat-quick-ref}
\end{table}

See Chapter~\ref{ch:cpu-architecture} (section on External Bus Signals) for usage examples including the PROT pin
interaction.
